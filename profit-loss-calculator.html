Business Margins Simplified

Knowing your exact profit margin is crucial for running a successful business. Profit happens when your Selling Price is greater than your Cost Price. Loss occurs when you sell a product for less than what you paid to buy or manufacture it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1. What is Cost Price?

Cost Price (CP) is the original price at which you bought the goods, including manufacturing, transport, and packaging costs.

Q2. How is Profit Margin Percentage calculated?

The formula used is: (Profit Amount ÷ Cost Price) × 100. This tells you how much percentage of return you made on your investment.

Q3. What does "Return on Invest (ROI) 1.5x" mean?

It means you made 1.5 times the money you initially spent. If you invested ₹1000 and sold for ₹1500, your money grew by 1.5x.
